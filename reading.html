Reading

Books I’m reading, plus one takeaway from each.
Make Your Bed
William H. McRaven

I’ve always liked things clean and orderly, and I probably get that from my mom. But after reading Make Your Bed, I gained a deeper appreciation for doing the small things right and for how much they actually matter.

What seems like a mundane task to most people, making your bed in the morning, often is not a priority. I get it. You set your alarm for 7:00, hit snooze a few times, and suddenly you are rushing just to shower and brush your teeth, let alone make the bed. But Admiral McCraven reframes that simple habit in a powerful way by emphasizing the importance of accomplishing the first task of the day.

That small win creates a sense of momentum. You carry that feeling of accomplishment forward and build on it throughout the day. And even if the day does not go as planned, you still come home to a made bed, a small but meaningful reminder that you did something right. It is a simple comfort, and one I have come to value even more.
