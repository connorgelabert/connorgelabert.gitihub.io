The Key to New Year’s: Consistency

January 2026

For my first blog, I want to talk about some of my New Year’s resolutions, especially given that it’s the beginning of 2026.

I’ve fallen victim many times, as I’m sure many of you have, to coming up with ambitious goals for the new year and then falling short within just a few weeks. Why does this happen to everyone? That initial wave of excitement and determination suddenly seems to fade away by the third or fourth week.

This year, one of my main goals was to be more consistent in the gym. So far, I’d say I’m off to a great start. I’ve had to make some adjustments with my new job, since I start work at 7:00 a.m. every morning.

I wake up at 4:45 a.m. and try to get to the gym around 5:00. It’s a sacrifice I’m more than happy to make, especially considering that the gym after work is a madhouse. In the mornings, it’s quiet, less busy, and you see the same 10 to 15 people every time.

Working out, for me, is a way to release stress, and there’s no better feeling than leaving the gym after a great workout. It’s the perfect way for me to start my day. That sense of accomplishment is something I can build on as the day goes on, a lesson I picked up from reading Make Your Bed.

I think, like most things, the key is consistency. You won’t see results after a week, or even after a month, but when you finally start to notice some differences, it makes you want to work even harder. Maybe that’s why people don’t last more than a few weeks with their goals. They aren’t willing to stay consistent, and they’re impatient.

I like to think of someone cutting down a tree with a saw. A few strokes won’t make much of an impact. Not even fifty strokes. But if you stay consistent, you will eventually achieve that goal.
