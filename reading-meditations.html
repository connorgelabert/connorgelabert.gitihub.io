Meditations

Marcus Aurelius

Meditations is a collection of private reflections written by one of the most powerful men in history, the Emperor of Rome. Across these entries, Aurelius returns to several core themes, including the importance of controlling one’s emotions, resisting the pull of fame and external praise, and accepting death as an unavoidable part of life.

The theme that resonated most with me is his belief that many problems are created in the mind. Our perception of an event, labeling it as “bad” or “stressful,” is often the real source of unhappiness. If you can control how you interpret what happens, you can keep your peace and respond with clarity instead of emotion.

When it comes to anxiety, he reminds you to stay anchored in the present and trust that you’ll handle the future when it arrives.

“Let not future things disturb you, for you will come to them, if it shall be necessary, having with you the same reason which you now use for present things.”
